How to start planning an ADU in the Greater Sacramento area

Most ADU headaches come from skipping early checks. Here’s the simple order that prevents expensive redesigns.

1) Identify constraints early

Start with setbacks, easements, slopes, access, trees, and existing structures. A clean concept on paper can fail fast if the site reality is ignored.

2) Utilities and service capacity

Water, sewer/septic, gas, and electrical service size can influence ADU placement and cost. Utility runs and panel upgrades are common cost drivers.

3) Choose the right ADU type

Detached vs attached vs garage conversion all have different tradeoffs. The “best” option depends on layout goals, site access, and what your jurisdiction allows.

4) Plan review and permits

Each city/county has its own process and timing. Getting clarity on submittal requirements and review cycles early helps you build a realistic schedule.

5) Lock scope before you price

Big cost swings usually come from scope changes after design starts. The more decisions you make early, the fewer surprises you get later.
